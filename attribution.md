# Attribution  
**SCU Foundation — Legal & Compliance**  
**Status:** Canonical (Pre‑Normative)  
**Maintained by:** SCU Foundation

This document defines how to properly attribute SCU Foundation materials, including governance documents, standards, publications, and institutional content.

Attribution ensures clarity, transparency, and accurate representation of the SCU Foundation’s work across academic, professional, and public‑sector contexts.

---

## 1. General Attribution Requirements

Unless otherwise stated, SCU Foundation materials may be:

- shared  
- referenced  
- cited  
- incorporated into academic or professional work  

Attribution must:

- clearly identify the SCU Foundation as the source  
- include the title of the document or standard  
- include the publication URL (when applicable)  
- avoid implying endorsement, partnership, or affiliation  

Attribution does **not** grant permission to use logos, identity marks, or branding elements.

---

## 2. Citing Governance Documents

Governance‑root documents (e.g., Governance Charter, Global Body Summary, Standards Registry) should be cited as:

**SCU Foundation. “Document Title.” scu.foundation/governance/...**

Example:

> SCU Foundation. “Governance Charter.” https://scu.foundation/governance/charter/

Governance documents are unversioned and evolve as part of the governance corpus.

---

## 3. Citing Standards and Specifications

Technical standards—including the Lattice Protocol, Lattice Web, Lattice Meta, Lattice Identity Token, and Trust Stack—should be cited using:

- the standard name  
- the edition or version number  
- the canonical URL  

Example:

> SCU Foundation. “Lattice Protocol v1.0.” https://latticeprotocol.org

When referencing specific sections, include section numbers or headings where appropriate.

---

## 4. Citing Publications

Publications such as the SCU Whitepaper v1.7, v1.8, and future editions should be cited as:

**SCU Foundation. “SCU Whitepaper vX.Y.” scu.foundation/publications/whitepaper/**

Example:

> SCU Foundation. “SCU Whitepaper v1.8.” https://scu.foundation/publications/whitepaper/

Publications are versioned and archived for long‑term reference.

---

## 5. Website Content

When citing content from **scu.foundation**, attribution should include:

- the page title  
- the URL  
- the SCU Foundation as the source  

Example:

> SCU Foundation. “Safety Disclosure.” https://scu.foundation/legal/safety-disclosure/

---

## 6. No Implicit Endorsement

Attribution must **not**:

- imply endorsement  
- imply partnership or affiliation  
- suggest that the SCU Foundation has reviewed or approved derivative work  

Attribution acknowledges the source; it does not grant institutional authority.

---

## 7. Logos and Identity Marks

SCU Foundation logos, identity marks, and branding elements:

- are **not** covered by CC BY 4.0  
- may **not** be used without written permission  
- may not be included in derivative works, marketing materials, or product documentation  

Requests for permission may be directed to **contact@scu.foundation**.

---

## 8. Third‑Party Materials

Some SCU Foundation documents reference or embed third‑party content.  
Such content must be attributed according to the licensing terms of the respective rights holders.

The SCU Foundation does not grant rights to third‑party materials.
