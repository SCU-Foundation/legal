# Security Disclosure  
**SCU Foundation — Legal & Compliance**  
**Status:** Canonical (Pre‑Normative)  
**Maintained by:** SCU Foundation

These guidelines describe how to responsibly report security‑related issues affecting SCU Foundation websites, publications, or infrastructure.

The SCU Foundation is committed to professionalism, confidentiality, and timely review of all security reports.

---

## 1. Scope

This Security Disclosure Policy applies to:

- the public website at **scu.foundation**  
- all subpaths under this domain  
- institutional publications  
- the HTML edition of the **SCU Whitepaper v1.8** and future editions  

This policy **does not apply** to external sites, including:

- **latticeprotocol.org**  
- **lattice.institute**  
- **p-sc.cc**  

External sites maintain their own security policies and disclosure channels.

---

## 2. Responsible Disclosure

The SCU Foundation welcomes responsible reporting of security vulnerabilities.

Reports are:

- reviewed promptly  
- handled confidentially  
- treated with respect for the reporter’s time and effort  
- evaluated according to severity and potential impact  

The Foundation does **not** operate a bug bounty program.  
Responsible disclosure is appreciated and contributes to the long‑term stability of the ecosystem.

---

## 3. How to Report a Security Issue

Security‑related issues may be reported via:

**Email:** contact@scu.foundation  
**Preferred Language:** English (en)  
**PGP / Encryption:** (If applicable, may be added in future editions)  
**Policy Expires:** 2026‑12‑31T23:59:00Z  

Please include:

- a clear description of the issue  
- steps to reproduce (if applicable)  
- any relevant technical details  
- your preferred contact method  

We request that reporters refrain from publicly disclosing vulnerabilities until the Foundation has had an opportunity to investigate and address the issue.

---

## 4. Canonical `security.txt`

The SCU Foundation publishes a machine‑readable `security.txt` file in accordance with **RFC 9116**.

You may view it at:

**https://scu.foundation/.well-known/security.txt**

This file contains the canonical contact information and disclosure metadata for automated tools and security researchers.

---

## 5. Out of Scope

The following are **not** considered security vulnerabilities:

- broken links  
- typographical errors  
- missing metadata  
- non‑sensitive server errors  
- issues on external sites not operated by the SCU Foundation  

For governance, versioning, and institutional principles, see the **Governance** section of the SCU Foundation website.

---

## 6. Contact

For all security‑related inquiries:

**Email:** contact@scu.foundation

This Security Disclosure Policy may be updated periodically to reflect:

- hosting changes  
- operational practices  
- legal or compliance requirements  

When material changes occur, the updated version will be published at this location.
