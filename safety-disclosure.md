# Safety Disclosure  
**SCU Foundation — Legal & Compliance**  
**Status:** Canonical (Pre‑Normative)  
**Maintained by:** SCU Foundation

This Safety Disclosure Policy describes how to report safety‑related concerns involving SCU Foundation websites, publications, or institutional materials.

The SCU Foundation is committed to responsible reporting, transparency, and long‑term public‑good stewardship.

---

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to:

- the public website at **scu.foundation**  
- all subpaths under this domain  
- institutional publications  
- the HTML edition of the **SCU Whitepaper v1.8** and future editions  

This policy **does not apply** to external sites or independent ecosystem bodies, including:

- **latticeprotocol.org**  
- **lattice.institute**  
- **p-sc.cc**  

External organisations maintain their own safety and reporting processes.

---

## 2. What Constitutes a Safety Concern

Safety concerns may include:

- misleading, outdated, or incorrect safety‑related information  
- issues in published materials that could cause misunderstanding or misuse  
- concerns about clarity, neutrality, or institutional framing  
- risks arising from documentation, examples, or explanatory text  
- accessibility or inclusivity issues in public materials  
- any content that may unintentionally create harm, confusion, or misinterpretation  

This policy does **not** cover security vulnerabilities.  
For security issues, see the **Security Disclosure Policy**.

---

## 3. Responsible Disclosure

The SCU Foundation welcomes responsible reporting of safety‑related issues.

Reports are:

- reviewed promptly  
- handled confidentially  
- evaluated according to potential impact  
- addressed through the Foundation’s governance and publication processes  

The Foundation does **not** operate a safety bounty program.  
Responsible disclosure is appreciated and contributes to the long‑term stability and clarity of the ecosystem.

---

## 4. How to Report a Safety Issue

Safety‑related concerns may be reported via:

**Email:** contact@scu.foundation  
**Preferred Language:** English (en)

Please include:

- a clear description of the concern  
- the page or document where it appears  
- any relevant context or examples  
- your preferred contact method (optional)  

Reporters are not required to provide personal information.

---

## 5. Out of Scope

The following are **not** considered safety issues under this policy:

- typographical errors  
- stylistic preferences  
- disagreements with governance decisions  
- issues on external sites not operated by the SCU Foundation  
- protocol‑level safety concerns (these belong to the Trust Stack and governance processes)  

For protocol‑level safety governance, see the **Safety Governance** document in the Governance repository.

---

## 6. Relationship to Governance

Safety concerns reported under this policy may inform:

- updates to institutional materials  
- clarifications in documentation  
- improvements to public‑facing explanations  
- governance‑root safety processes (where applicable)  

This policy does **not** define technical safety requirements.  
Those are governed by the **Trust Stack** and the **Safety Governance** corpus.

---

## 7. Updates to This Policy

This Safety Disclosure Policy may be updated periodically to reflect:

- changes in infrastructure  
- governance updates  
- operational practices  
- legal or compliance requirements  

When material changes occur, the updated version will be published at this location.
